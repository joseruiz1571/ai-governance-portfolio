## About

This repository collects the applied governance work I produce as part of my practice in AI risk management. The focus is concrete: realistic systems, documented sources, framework-mapped findings, and recommendations a Legal, Security, Privacy, or Business stakeholder can act on.

I come to this work from twenty years in library and information science. Librarianship has been wrestling with the ethics of classification, authority, and equitable access to information for a century. AI governance is the same set of questions asked at a different scale — which is the lens I bring to every piece here.

I also write about this work at **[Controlled Vocabulary](https://controlledvocabulary.substack.com)** — applied risk assessments, threat-to-control mapping, and library-science parallels for AI policy.

## Projects

### Summary of Compliance Risk for the BK Assistant Algorithm
A compliance risk analysis of Burger King's OpenAI-powered drive-through employee monitoring system, piloting at ~500 U.S. locations including Chicago and Schaumburg, IL.

- **Primary issues:** BIPA voiceprint exposure; minor-employee governance gap
- **Frameworks referenced:** Illinois BIPA (740 ILCS 14); EU AI Act Article 5(1)(f) (workplace affect-inference prohibition)
- **File:** [`bk-assistant.md`](./bk-assistant.md)

### Due Diligence Assessment of Anthropic's Claude Code
A vendor risk assessment of Claude Code for potential internal deployment in a SaaS software development context. Scores seven risk dimensions, maps findings to NIST AI RMF functions, and produces a risk register with a conditional-approval decision.

- **Primary issues:** Execute DPA; restrict agentic scope; gate on CVE-2026-21852 patch
- **Frameworks referenced:** NIST AI RMF (Govern / Map / Measure / Manage); ISO/IEC 42001; ISO/IEC 27001; EU AI Act GPAI; SOC 2
- **File:** [`claude-code.md`](./claude-code.md)
